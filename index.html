What is Gastrofest Batumi

One city.
Many tables.
One festival.

Gastrofest is a gastronomic festival format that has been running successfully in Belarus since 2016. We're bringing it to Batumi — for the first time in the city, and likely the first time in Georgia.

The idea is simple. A selection of the city's restaurants, bars, and cafés each prepare one exclusive tasting set — a signature combination of food and drink, created specifically for the festival period. Every set is available at the same fixed price across all participating venues. Guests choose where to go, show up, and order. No tickets. No passes. No complicated rules.

The festival runs for a limited time — typically two to three weeks. During that time, the city becomes something to discover. Not just your usual three spots, but twenty places you maybe never tried, each showing you their best.

The first Gastrofest Batumi event will be a coffee festival — around 15–20 of the city's coffee shops, each offering one special coffee drink and a dessert made for the occasion, all at 25 lari.

For Restaurants & Bars

Why participate

Gastrofest isn't a one-time traffic spike. It's an introduction between your venue and people who didn't know you yet.

New guests who actually show up

Batumi has a lot of places to eat and drink. Most people rotate through the same handful of spots. Gastrofest disrupts that habit — it gives people a specific, low-risk reason to try somewhere new. Your venue gets in front of an audience that was never going to find you through Instagram alone.

Promotion you don't have to build yourself

Every participating venue gets a dedicated page on the Gastrofest Batumi website with your set, your details, and your story. You're featured in all festival announcements, social media posts, and communications to our audience. The promotional infrastructure is already there.

A real marketing moment, not just a discount

This isn't a delivery app deal where you cut your margins and hope for volume. You're designing one special set — something that represents your venue at its best. It's a creative exercise as much as a commercial one.

Honest feedback from real guests

The voting mechanic means guests are actively paying attention to what you offer. You'll see how people respond to your set, read the reactions, and understand what lands. That kind of direct feedback is hard to get otherwise.

You're part of something the city hasn't seen before

This is the first event of this kind in Batumi. The venues that participate in the first edition are the ones who helped build it. That's a different kind of visibility than joining something that already exists.

A note on how participation works: The number of participating venues per edition is limited. Not every application will be accepted — sets go through a light review process to make sure the festival maintains a consistent standard. This isn't to be exclusive for the sake of it. It's to make sure that when guests show up, they have a genuinely good experience — which is what brings them back to your venue after the festival ends.

For Guests

What's in it for you

A festival where the whole city is the venue.

Discover places you've been meaning to try

Gastrofest is a good excuse to finally go — and a comfortable one, because you already know what each place is offering and exactly what it costs before you walk in.

A set made specifically for the festival

Every participating venue creates something exclusive for Gastrofest — not a random item from the regular menu, but a combination put together specifically for this occasion.

Fixed price, everywhere

Every set costs the same fixed price across all participating venues. No guessing, no bill anxiety, no "let me check if I can afford this place." You already know.

Go once or go ten times

There's no pass to buy and no ticket to collect. Come for one venue or make a proper run of it — visit five coffee shops in a week, compare, form opinions.

Vote for your favorites

At the end of your visit, you get to vote for the sets you loved. At the close of the festival, we announce the top venues. It makes the whole experience feel like it means something.
